Reversing Adverse Changes from Human Settlement: The De-extinction Project

Little boy touches a mammoth carcass outside the Shemanovsky Museum in Russia

Click image below to Enlarge.

With modern advancement in technology, there has been some hope in reversing the adverse changes to nature from human settlement in North America. In 2014, genetic scientists have discovered an innovated technology to revitalize the extinct populations such as the North American Passenger pigeons. The research is called “The De-extinction” Project or resurrection biology. The process includes creating extinct species by cloning and breeding genetically altered present-day species that are either members or resemble the extinct said specie. This newly formed technology would allow extinct animals (or plants) to be bought to life and repopulated. However, there is significant scientific and ethical controversy about this biological science. Regardless, scientists believe these genetic transformations will soon be available for implementation. Nevertheless, the execution of these controversial advances in science are yet to occur and are still in theory.
